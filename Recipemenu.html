Classic Shakshuka

Eggs gently poached in a bold, spiced tomato and pepper sauce — smoky, hearty, and made entirely in one pan. The breakfast that turns any morning into an occasion.

★★★★★ 4.9 (312 reviews) · LM By Laila Mansour
⏱️

🔥

👥
Servings 4 people
📊

What You'll Need
Ingredients
Adjust servings
4
The Sauce Base
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans crushed tomatoes (400g each)
Spices
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
To Finish
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g feta cheese, crumbled
  • handful fresh parsley or coriander
  • 4 slices crusty bread, for serving
Let's Get Cooking
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Soften the aromatics
Heat the olive oil in a large, deep skillet or cast-iron pan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sliced red pepper. Cook for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is translucent and the pepper has softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1 minute until fragrant.
Use a wide pan — you'll need space for the eggs later.
2
Build the spiced sauce
Add the cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne, and salt directly to the pan. Stir and cook for 1 minute until the spices are toasted and fragrant. Pour in both cans of crushed tomatoes and stir everything together. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
Taste the sauce now — adjust salt and cayenne to your liking before adding the eggs.
3
Poach the eggs
Using the back of a spoon, create 4 small wells in the sauce. Crack one egg carefully into each well. Season the eggs lightly with salt and pepper. Cover the pan with a lid and cook for 5–7 minutes — 5 minutes for runny yolks, 7 minutes for fully set. Keep an eye on them as they can overcook quickly.
4
Finish and garnish
Remove the pan from the heat. Scatter crumbled feta cheese generously over the top. Finish with a handful of freshly chopped parsley or coriander. Drizzle with a little extra olive oil if you like a richer finish.
5
Serve immediately
Bring the pan straight to the table — shakshuka is best served hot, right from the skillet. Serve with thick crusty bread or warm pita for scooping up the sauce. Add a side of labneh or extra feta if you're feeling indulgent.
Shakshuka waits for no one — serve it the moment it's ready.

👩‍🍳 Chef's Notes

Make it your own: Add crumbled merguez sausage, chickpeas, or a handful of spinach to the sauce before adding the eggs.

Storage: The tomato sauce keeps well in the fridge for 3 days. Reheat and add fresh eggs when ready to serve.

Spice level: Omit the cayenne entirely for a mild version, or double it for serious heat.
